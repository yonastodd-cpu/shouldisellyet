5 What this actually means for you

Plain English, no hedging.

The –7.1% headline is mostly an illusion for houses like yours. 20906 includes a large stock of condos (especially around Leisure World), and more of them sold this year — dragging the ZIP's "typical price" down while price per square foot rose 8.6%. A single-family home in good condition is not down 7%.

But three things deserve your attention: homes take ~12 more days to sell than last year, listings are up 31%, and the DC region's federal job losses are still working through the market. If you're thinking of selling in the next 12–18 months, the data says sooner beats later — and pricing correctly on day one matters more than any year since 2019.
